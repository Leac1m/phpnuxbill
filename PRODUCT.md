# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

ISP technicians, network administrators, and hotspot owners who manage MikroTik routers, customer accounts, billing, and internet plans. They're in a work context — at a desk or on-site — managing infrastructure that must stay reliable. They come to PHPNuxBill to provision service, track usage, collect payments, and resolve issues fast.

## Product Purpose

PHPNuxBill is a billing management system for MikroTik hotspots and PPPoE connections. It exists to make ISP operations simple, reliable, and professional — turning a complex technical workflow into a clean, efficient interface. Success means the admin can manage their entire business from one panel without friction.

## Brand Personality

Professional, Modern, Dependable. The tone is confident but not cold — authoritative without being corporate. It should feel like a well-built tool from a company that takes its craft seriously. Not flashy, not trendy; just solid and well-considered.

## Anti-references

- Generic Bootstrap admin templates. No cookie-cutter sidebar-with-cards patterns that make every tool look interchangeable.
- Overly dark "hacker terminal" aesthetic. Network tools often default to dark-edgy — this should stay bright, clear, and approachable.
- Cluttered dashboards with too many widgets competing for attention.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over density.** Every screen should be scannable in seconds. White space is a feature, not wasted space.
2. **Confidence without noise.** The interface earns trust through precision — aligned grids, consistent spacing, deliberate color — not through decoration or effects.
3. **Professional warmth.** Not cold corporate, not casual. The tone is respectful of the user's expertise without being sterile.
4. **Brand as experience.** The billing panel isn't just a tool — it's the daily workspace of its users. Every pixel should feel intentional.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard web best practices: readable body text (≥4.5:1 contrast), keyboard-navigable interfaces, clear focus indicators, and reduced-motion support for animations. No formal WCAG compliance target, but zero egregious failures.
